About

I am a research fellow at the Korea Institute for Advanced Study (KIAS), affiliated with the Center for AI and Natural Sciences. My research lies at the interface of theoretical high-energy physics and machine learning, where I use modern neural architectures — in particular transformer models — to attack inverse problems in the AdS/CFT correspondence.

Broadly, I am interested in what holography can teach us about strongly coupled quantum matter, and conversely how data-driven methods can uncover dual geometries, reconstruct bulk spacetimes from boundary data, and probe phase structure in models of holographic superconductivity. Earlier in my career I worked on perturbative holographic renormalization, supersymmetric Janus solutions, and axion phenomenology.

Before joining KIAS, I was a senior researcher at Kookmin University (2022–2025) and at GIST (2021–2022), and a visiting graduate researcher at CERN in 2019. I earned my Ph.D. in Physics from Kyung Hee University in 2021.

Research interests Generative AI · Machine Learning · Inverse Problems · Holography · AdS/CFT · Gauge/Gravity Duality · String Theory · Supergravity
